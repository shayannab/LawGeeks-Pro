Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

At LawGeeks, your privacy is our priority. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, protect, and share your information when you use our AI-powered legal document analysis platform.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

  • Account Information: Name, email address, password, and company name
  • Document Content: Legal documents you upload for analysis
  • Payment Information: Billing details processed securely through Stripe (we never store full credit card numbers)
  • Communication Data: Messages you send us through support channels

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Usage Data: Pages visited, features used, time spent, documents analyzed
  • Device Information: IP address, browser type, operating system
  • Cookies: Session data, preferences, and analytics (see our Cookie Policy)

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To provide and improve our AI document analysis service
  • To communicate with you about your account and our services
  • To process payments and prevent fraud
  • To personalize your experience and provide recommendations
  • To analyze usage patterns and improve our AI models
  • To send marketing communications (with your consent)
  • To comply with legal obligations

3. How We Protect Your Documents

Your documents are sensitive. Here's how we protect them:

  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest
  • Access Controls: Strict role-based access for our team
  • Temporary Storage: Documents are deleted 30 days after upload unless you save them
  • No Training by Default: We do NOT use your documents to train our AI models without explicit consent
  • SOC 2 Compliance: Independently audited security standards

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do NOT sell your personal information. We share data only in these limited cases:

  • Service Providers: Cloud hosting (AWS), payment processing (Stripe), analytics (anonymized)
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, court order, or government request
  • Business Transfers: In case of merger, acquisition, or sale of assets
  • With Your Consent: When you explicitly authorize sharing

5. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of all data we have about you
  • Correction: Update inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your account and all associated data
  • Portability: Download your data in a machine-readable format
  • Opt-Out: Unsubscribe from marketing emails at any time
  • Object: Object to processing of your data for certain purposes

To exercise these rights, email us at privacy@lawgeeks.com

6. Data Retention

  • Account Data: Retained while your account is active
  • Documents: Deleted 30 days after upload (unless saved by you)
  • Analysis Results: Retained for 90 days
  • Backups: Deleted within 90 days of account closure

7. International Data Transfers

LawGeeks is based in the United States. If you access our service from outside the US, your data may be transferred to and processed in the US. We use Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission to protect data transferred from the EU.

8. Children's Privacy

LawGeeks is not intended for users under 18. We do not knowingly collect information from children. If you believe we have collected data from a child, contact us immediately.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We'll notify you of significant changes via email or a prominent notice on our website. Continued use of our service after changes constitutes acceptance.

10. Contact Us

  • Mail: LawGeeks Inc., 123 Legal Tech Blvd, San Francisco, CA 94105
  • Privacy Officer: privacy@lawgeeks.com

11. GDPR (European Users)

If you're in the EU/EEA, you have additional rights under GDPR:

  • The right to lodge a complaint with your supervisory authority
  • The right to withdraw consent at any time
  • The right to restrict processing of your data

Our Data Protection Officer: dpo@lawgeeks.com

12. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

California residents have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we collect and how we use it
  • Request deletion of personal information
  • Opt-out of the sale of personal information (we do not sell data)
  • Non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights
